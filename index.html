The Dark Knight

"I like it a lot."

-Taegen Robinson

The Dark knight was a great film with a perfect mix of action and suspense. Not only did it keep you on your toes, but it could also make you laugh. The acting duo of Christian Bale and Heath Ledger was enough to keep my eyes glued to screen.

Christian Bale in back and Heath Ledger in front

The Dark Knight had both a great plot and great actors. I belive that it is the best of the Batman Trilogy and may be the best for a long time.

Plot:

Batman has cleaned up the streets and Gotham is near crimeless, but then The Joker shows up and things go bizzare. The Joker is killing people, little at a time, until the Bat reveals his identity. At a press conference Bruce Wayne makes a plan to reveal that he is Batman. But Harvey Dent wants the Bat to stay alive so he claims that he is Batman. Joker now thinks that Harvey Dent is Batman and tries to kill him but when the real Batman comes in he is able to take down The Joker and bring him into prison. While the Joker is in prison, his goons capture Harvey Dent and Rachel and trap them. The two hostages are tied to a bunch of oil barrels and a bomb is about to explode. Batman goes to save Rachel but got the wrong address and ends up saving Harvey. Although, half of Harvey's face gets lit on fire and he turns into Two Face. Joker escaped prison and he keeps all of Gotham hostage on two different boats. Batman sets up a system that tracks the Joker and stops him. Meanwhile, Commisioner Gordon's family gets kidnapped by Two Face and Batman has to break his one rule and kill Two Face to stop him. After that Batman runs away and isn't seen for a long time.

Rating PG-13

Violence & Gore

Several scenes where people are shot, although it either cuts away or little blood is shown.

In one scene The Joker impales another person with a pencil but it is not shown.

The Joker slashes another man's cheeks but the camera cuts away.

A dead person is shown hanging from a noose in a "jump" scene.

Several scenes of mild violence where there are fist fights and guns. Sometimes dogs are used.

There are several explosions where people are killed but it is not shown.

Two-Face's face is very graphic.

Profanity

Some adult language.

Alcohol/Drugs/Smoking

Several scenes where there is drinking.

A mobster is constantly smoking a cigar.

In the beginning a drug dealer is meeting up with someone to deliver something but the drug isn't shown and the meeting gets broken up.

Frightening/Intense Scenes

The Joker threatens to blow up a hospital.

Two-Face threatens to kill Gordon's family.

The Joker posts a chilling video of himself where he has a hostage and appears crazy.

Overall this movie should be ok for ages 10 and up.
